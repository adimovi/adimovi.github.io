The first thing we need to point out here is that even though we refer to nurse companions, they are actually qualified nurses aides, and not fully qualified nurses.
We can also provide fully qualified nurses, if required by a client.

Could a nurse companion become a dating companion?

Of course that is a possibility, but for sure not expected and any client who requires a nurse companion is obligated to provide a separate bedroom.

What are the obligations of a live-in nurse companion?

Unlike other live-in nurse providers, we expect our nurses to be more like a companion, which means they are happy to attend social functions with our clients or go on excursions, as requested, as well as look after the home and whatever medical needs they are qualified to execute.
We look for good empathic natured nurse companions who have a very warm and positive type of attitude to life.

Is there usually an age difference between clients and dating/nursing companions?

We try to keep both categories of companions between the ages of 40 to 50 years of age, because more maturity = less potential hassle, and a much better chance of settling into a comfortable way of life together.

At what point do either dating or nursing companions meet clients?

We introduce all companions to clients on video calls before they ever arrive in Asia, and clients are able to meet a few this way before they select the one they think best suits them. Clients can also ask for a companion to be replaced at any time, but that works both ways, and a companion can also ask for a different client, which is why it is important to treat a good companion properly and with respect. Of course it's easier for a client to find another companion than the other way round, but all concerned will reap what they sow, as in any situation in life.
